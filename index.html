Each tile shows the latest World Bank value (year shown) with a mini trend and its exact source indicator code, so you always know where a number comes from. Hover a trend to read any year. Metrics confirmed to have no data for a country are hidden (others show “loading…” until fetched). “Average income” = GNI per capita (PPP); money converts from international/US dollars at current market rates. Govt debt has thin coverage (e.g. China is missing — that needs IMF data, coming with the multi-source update). For the US, a party-coloured head-of-state timeline is shown, and hovering a trend also labels each year with the president & party. Top-5 export/import products and sub-industry breakdowns need product-level trade data (Harvard Atlas / OEC), which arrives with the multi-source snapshot. Benchmark: each tile shows where the country stands versus a reference — its percentile and how it compares to the population-weighted average (the “typical person”), switchable between World, its region, and its World Bank income group. On each trend, the dashed orange line is that reference through time, so you can see a country converging toward or pulling away from it. Reference trend lines load progressively and are cached.
